Home Fire Safety Tips

[PLACEHOLDER: Brief intro about the importance of home fire prevention.]

Test Smoke Alarms Monthly

Install smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside bedrooms, and outside sleeping areas. Test them monthly and replace batteries at least once a year. Replace the entire unit every 10 years.

Keep a Fire Extinguisher in the Kitchen

Install at least one fire extinguisher in your kitchen and know how to use it. Remember: PASS — Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep. Never use water on a grease fire.

Plan and Practice Your Escape

Create a home fire escape plan with two exits from every room. Choose an outdoor meeting place and practice the plan at least twice a year — especially with children.

Never Leave Cooking Unattended

Cooking is the leading cause of home fires. Stay in the kitchen when frying, grilling, or broiling. Keep flammable items — towels, curtains, and packaging — away from the stove.

Home Safety Checklist

Use this checklist to assess your home's fire safety readiness.

Smoke & CO Alarms

  • Smoke alarm on every level of the home
  • Smoke alarm inside each bedroom
  • Carbon monoxide alarm near sleeping areas
  • Batteries tested within the last month
  • Units are less than 10 years old

Escape Planning

  • Two exits identified from every room
  • Escape plan drawn and posted
  • Outdoor meeting point established
  • Escape plan practiced in the last 6 months
  • Children know to crawl low under smoke

Kitchen Safety

  • Fire extinguisher mounted and accessible
  • Members of household know how to use it
  • Stove area kept clear of flammables
  • Never leave cooking unattended

Electrical & Heating

  • No frayed or damaged electrical cords
  • Outlets not overloaded
  • Space heaters kept 3 feet from flammables
  • Chimney inspected within the last year
